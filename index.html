the founding story

Every great company starts with two people who shouldn't have been in the same room.

DAY 0
The hackathon
Forty hours, too much caffeine, and somewhere between a broken build and a 4am demo run-through, I noticed the most dangerous thing in any hackathon: someone more focused on the problem than the prize. That was you.
SEED ROUND
The first real conversation
Not about code. Not about the project. Just you, talking about an idea you were stubborn enough to believe in before anyone else did. I remember thinking: this one's going to build something. Turns out you built into me too.
SERIES A
Watching you become an entrepreneur, for real
The late nights that weren't hackathon nights anymore — they were yours. Your own deadlines, your own bets, your own name on the line. I've never had a front row seat to someone working that hard for something that's actually theirs.
TODAY

founder's letter

For Shubhika, on her birthday

Happy birthday, Shubhika.

I won't pretend this makes up for not being there. It doesn't. But I wanted today to have at least one thing in it that was built just for you, from scratch, the way you build everything.

You met me at a hackathon — the one place where I was supposed to be impressing strangers with a project, and instead I walked away more interested in one person than any idea on that floor. I think about that a lot. How the best thing that ever happened to me showed up disguised as a coding event.

Watching you become who you are now — stubborn in the right ways, building things out of nothing, betting on yourself before anyone else would — it's the most quietly inspiring thing I get to witness. You don't need today to be perfect. You've never needed things handed to you. But you deserve, at the very least, to feel how much you're loved on it.

So: happy birthday. I'm not there, but every part of me wishes I was. Soon, I promise.
— always your co-founder in this, wherever I am
